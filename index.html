Incredible India: Tourist Destinations

Taj Mahal, Agra

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the right bank of the river Yamuna in Agra. It was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan to house the tomb of his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal. The tomb is the centerpiece of a 17-hectare complex, which includes a mosque and a guest house, and is set in formal gardens bounded on three sides by a crenellated wall.

Best time to visit: October to March

Jaipur, Rajasthan

Jaipur is the capital of India's Rajasthan state. It's known as the "Pink City" due to the distinctive color of its buildings. The city was founded in 1727 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II, who was the ruler of Amber. The city's colorful, chaotic streets are a mix of modern and ancient architecture, creating a vibrant and unique atmosphere.

Best time to visit: November to February

Kerala Backwaters

The Kerala backwaters are a network of interconnected canals, rivers, lakes, and inlets formed by more than 900 km of waterways. These waterways have become a prominent tourist attraction, with many tourists taking multi-day houseboat cruises. The backwaters are home to unique aquatic life and offer a glimpse into the rural lifestyle of Kerala.

Best time to visit: September to March

Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh

Varanasi, also known as Banaras or Kashi, is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world. Situated on the banks of the River Ganges, Varanasi is considered one of the holiest cities in India. The city is known for its ghats (steps leading to the river), where pilgrims perform ritual ablutions and where cremation ceremonies take place.

Best time to visit: October to March
